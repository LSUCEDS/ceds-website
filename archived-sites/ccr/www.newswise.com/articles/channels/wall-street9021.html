Washington University in St. Louis

Both the Occupy and Tea Party movements, however, are reminders of how the right to assemble has been at the heart of some of the most important social movements in American history: antebellum abolitionism, womens suffrage and the Civil Rights Movement, says John Inazu, JD, PhD, associate professor of law at Washington University in St. Louis. In his new book, Libertys Refuge: The Forgotten Freedom of Assembly, recently published by Yale University Press, Inazu examines why freedom of assembly has become a historical footnote in American law and political theory, and what has been lost with the weakening of protections for private groups.
