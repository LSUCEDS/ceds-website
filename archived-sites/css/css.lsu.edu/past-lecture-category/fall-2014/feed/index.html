Catherine Seavitt
Principal
Catherine Seavitt Studio
Associate Professor of Landscape Architecture
City College of New York
Prof. Seavitt will speak on Structures of Coastal Resiliency: Jamaica Bay. Her work focuses on design adaptation to sea level rise in urban coastal environments and explores novel landscape restoration practices given the dynamics of climate change. She co-authored the book On the Water: Palisade Bay, a climate adaptation proposal for the New York / New Jersey Upper Harbor. This study examines the use of “soft” infrastructural systems to mitigate the impact of storm surge and was the foundation of the 2010 exhibition Rising Currents at the Museum of Modern Art. She is currently leading research at Jamaica Bay as part of the Rockefeller Foundation-funded grant Structures of Coastal Resilience. She earned her MArch from Princeton University.

John Anderson
Academic Director
Shell Center for Sustainability
W. Maurice Ewing Professor of Oceanography
Rice University
Dr. Anderson will discuss Accelerated Sea Level Rise and Impact on the Louisiana and Texas Coasts. His current research interests are (1) the recent retreat history of the Antarctic Ice Sheet and controls on ice sheet stability as it relates to sea level rise, and (2) the evolution of the Gulf Coast and response of coastal environments to climate change. He has participated in 24 scientific expeditions to Antarctica and has authored and co-authored over 230 peer-reviewed publications, edited 5 volumes, and published 2 books, Antarctic Marine Geology and Formation and Future of the Upper Texas Coast. His awards include Outstanding Educator of the Gulf Coast Geological Society and the 2007 Shepard Medal of the Society for Sedimentary Research. He earned his PhD from Florida State University.

Sean Duffy, Sr.
Executive Director
Big River Coalition
Mr. Duffy will discuss The Importance of the Mississippi River for World Trade and Sediment Recycling. He leads the Big River Coalition, which represents the navigation industry’s economic interests across the entire Mississippi River Basin and spearheaded the effort to deepen the river to 50 feet. Mr. Duffy is also the Executive Vice President of the Louisiana Maritime Association. His efforts to overhaul the Harbor Maintenance Trust Fund mechanism have earned him national recognition. He is keenly aware of the obstacles faced by the maritime industry, both nationally and those specific to Louisiana, and advises on coastal restoration and the beneficial use of dredged material. He is the third generation of his family employed in the maritime industry.

Mark your calendars, the CSS Brown Bag Lecture Series returns this fall! Speakers from the worlds of design, science, and engineering visit LSU to share their thoughts and ideas and encourage debate on a wide range of coastal topics. So bring your lunch, grab a seat, and enjoy the lively discussion. It’s FREE and OPEN TO ALL. We hope you will join us.

Wed, Oct 1     Noon-1:00pm
Allison Anderson
Principal
unabridged Architecture
Allison Anderson will speak on the subject of Civic Immunology. Anderson is recognized for civic projects that are carefully crafted and inherently defensible against climate challenges. After Hurricane Katrina devastated her community, her designs for recovery focused on sustainability, adaptation, and resilience. Her firm, unabridged Architecture, designs with an understanding of the importance of place and tradition within the modern context. She was the first LEED-Accredited Professional in Mississippi in 2002 and chairs the U.S. Green Building Council / Mississippi Advocacy Committee. She has taught architecture at the University of Texas, LSU, and Tulane. She earned her MArch at the University of Texas where she was awarded the President’s Fellowship.

Mr. Duffy is also the Executive Vice President of the Louisiana Maritime Association. His efforts to overhaul the Harbor Maintenance Trust Fund mechanism have earned him national recognition. He is keenly aware of the obstacles faced by the maritime industry, both nationally and those specific to Louisiana, and advises on coastal restoration and the beneficial use of dredged material. He is the third generation of his family employed in the maritime industry.

Virginia Burkett
Chief Scientist for Climate and Land Use Change
U.S. Geological Survey
Dr. Burkett will discuss the subject of Climate Change: Trends, Projections, and Implications for Coastal Louisiana. She has published extensively on the topics of global change and low-lying coastal zones and was lead author of the United Nations’ Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change Third, Fourth, and Fifth Assessment Reports, as well as all three U.S. National Climate Assessments. She was formerly Chief of the Forest Ecology Branch at the USGS National Wetlands Research Center in Lafayette, and also served as Secretary/Director of the Louisiana Department of Wildlife and Fisheries, Acting Director of the Louisiana Coastal Zone Management Program, and Assistant Director of the Louisiana Geological Survey. She earned her PhD in Forestry at Stephen F. Austin State University in Nacogdoches, Texas.
